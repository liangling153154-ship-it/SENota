# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Independent foreign travellers — mostly backpackers and self-guided road-trippers — planning a trip to Cao Bang, the far-northern highlands of Vietnam. They are researching on a phone, often mid-trip, comparing where to sleep, how to get a bike, which tour to take, and how to leave town by bus. They want a single trustworthy local who can arrange the whole stay so they can stop coordinating and start riding. The decisive action is a WhatsApp message to Sen, the host.

## Product Purpose

A single-host landing site for Sen's Homestay in Cao Bang that turns a curious traveller into a WhatsApp booking. It presents everything Sen offers — rooms, motorbike rental, guided tours, breakfast & laundry, bus tickets, a food guide, a route map — and makes the next step (message Sen) obvious from every section. Success = the visitor trusts Sen enough to send that first message. The site is the product: the visitor's impression of warmth, honesty, and local competence is the thing being made.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, and dependable — the voice of a real local host, not a hotel chain. Three words: **trustworthy, warm, real**. Copy is plain and candid (it even names the trade-offs: thin soundproofing, no TV). The emotional goal is the feeling of arriving at a friend's home in the mountains: safe, looked-after, and quietly excited for the road ahead. Adventure is present but it's the highland *backdrop*, not a loud extreme-sports pitch.

## Anti-references

- **Booking.com / Agoda / generic OTA UI** — cold, industrial, endless identical cards, no human behind it. This is the opposite of Sen.
- **Glossy 5-star resort sites** — fake luxury, gold-on-black, aspirational gloss. This is a real homestay; pretending otherwise breaks the honesty that is the brand.
- **"AI landing page" defaults** — cream/sand body background, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, paper-grain noise filter, rows of identical icon+heading+text cards, reflex fonts (Playfair / Lora / Inter / DM Sans). If it reads as machine-generated, it has failed.
- **Cold minimalism** — stark white, lots of empty space, no human or highland warmth. Restraint here reads as absence, not refinement.

## Design Principles

- **Honesty is the feature.** The brand wins trust by being candid (real prices, real trade-offs, real photos). Never over-promise; let plain truth do the selling.
- **One host, one thread.** Every path leads to a WhatsApp message to Sen. The site reduces coordination, it doesn't add a booking funnel.
- **Photos carry the place.** Cao Bang's waterfalls, mountains, and rooms do the persuading. Imagery leads; copy supports. A colored block where a photo belongs is a bug.
- **Mobile is the real product.** Visitors are on a phone, often on patchy highland data. Readable text, big tap targets, fast load, and graceful image fallbacks are non-negotiable.
- **Local, not templated.** The look should feel hand-made and specific to a highland homestay — earth, craft, warmth — never like a stock landing template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1); all interactive targets ≥44×44px; visible keyboard focus; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored with a non-animated fallback; meaningful `alt` text on imagery; no information conveyed by color alone. Audience is international (non-native English readers), so copy stays plain and the layout stays predictable; future localization should not break the layout.
