Rider's Guide · Semi-Automatic

How to ride a
semi-auto bike

No hand clutch — easier than a manual, safer on mountain roads.

🏍️ Wave Alpha · Future 125 · Yamaha PG-1 5-step guide
1

Know Your Controls

Before you start — understand what each hand and foot does

The 4 controls — left hand, right hand, left foot, right foot

🤚 Left Hand — DO NOTHING

No lever on this side. Not a brake, not a clutch. Keep it relaxed on the grip.

✋ Right Hand — Throttle + Brake

Twist back = gas. Squeeze lever = front brake.

🦶 Left Foot — Gear Shifter

Lift toe up = shift UP (N→1→2→3→4)
Press heel down = shift DOWN

🦵 Right Foot — Rear Brake

Press down to slow. Your primary brake — use it more than the front.

Left foot: lift toe = shift up · press heel = shift down
Right foot: press down to apply rear brake
2

The Golden Rule

Master this 3-step rhythm — every gear change, every time

⚠️

If you don't release the gas while shifting…

The bike will jerk forward violently. Always release the throttle completely before clicking the gear lever.

1
Release
Let go of throttle completely. Zero gas.
2
Shift
Click gear lever with left foot. One click.
3
Throttle
Gently twist gas again to continue.
3

Which Gear?

Match your gear to your speed

GearSpeedWhen to use
N0 km/hStarting, waiting at traffic lights
10–15 km/hMoving off, very slow / tight turns
215–30 km/hTown roads, slow traffic
330–50 km/hOpen roads, light traffic
450+ km/hHighways, long straights
💡

Listen to the engine

If the engine sounds strained or is revving very high — shift up. If it feels sluggish or struggles uphill — shift down.

4

Braking & Downhills

The most important safety skill in the mountains

🦵

Right foot first. Always apply the rear brake first and gradually. It's gentler and more stable than the front.

✋

Front brake second. Squeeze gently at the same time. Never grab suddenly — it can lock the wheel.

⛰️

Long downhills. Shift down to a lower gear. Let the engine help brake — don't ride the brakes the whole way.

🌧️

Wet roads. Brake earlier, more gently. The road surface in Cao Bang can be slippery after rain.

5

Practice First

10 minutes in the yard before heading to the mountains

1️⃣

Start in neutral. Feel the bike's weight. Get comfortable sitting.

2️⃣

Practice the 3-step rhythm at walking pace — release, shift, throttle.

3️⃣

Try smooth stops using both brakes together. No sudden grabs.

4️⃣

Go up through all 4 gears in a straight line, then back down. Repeat until it feels natural.
